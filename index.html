Nutrition

Nutrition – is the process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth.

Eating healthy is just making sure that you provide yourself with a balanced diet that contains all the seven factors that you need to stay fit or grow stronger. These seven factors include carbs, proteins, fats, fibre, vitamins, minerals and water. A lot of these will already be in your diet but the hard part is knowing which are good and bad and need to be cut down or increased. Although, you don’t want too much or too little of these you need to find the right equilibrium for your body. As a performing artist, the proportions will change slightly as we need more slow-burning carbs to get us through a full day of training and more proteins to rebuild and strengthen our muscle fibres that have ripped. Additionally, a healthy diet can help prevent us from injury, reducing fatigue and increase our energy levels and therefore our performance. If we as performers neglect our diets then we will be on the back foot of the competitive industry that we are trying to get hired in. Just by not having enough carbs you will suffer from a lack of energy and attentiveness that panels will be looking for as soon as you walk in the door for an audition, losing you the job before you’ve even started to showcase your talent. A lot of this information can be found online but not all of it is reliable and factual. The best way to find reliable information is the find reputable sources (such as national health organisations or scientific papers focused on the performing arts or athletes), you then take the information you gather from these sources and cross-reference them by comparing them to each other to see if the information matches up, then you’ll start to know in the information you’ve learnt in reliable.

Superfoods

A superfood a nutrient-rich food considered to be especially beneficial for health and well-being. Here are some examples:

  1. Berries
    • Such as Raspberries, strawberries, blueberries and blackberries are classed as superfoods as they’re all high in vitamins, minerals, fibre and antioxidants. Blueberries have also shown in studies that they may reduce the risk of certain heart conditions from their nutrients which can be found in other berries too.
    • Berries can be eaten simply as a snack by themself or even sprinkled on top of some cereal or yoghurt
  2. Whole Grains
    • These can be a good source of the fibres you need; it has several B vitamins and minerals. Whole grain has also been shown to lower cholesterol and protect against heart disease and diabetes.
    • You can substitute lots of carbs for whole grain alternatives, such as bread, you can replace potatoes with quinoa, brown rice etc.
  3. Nuts and Seeds
    • Nuts are a great source of protein and mono-saturated fats which can help reduce the risk of heart attacks. Some can also contain properties of anti-inflammatory and antioxidants, although nuts can be dense in calories so eating ones with shells can slow you down so you don’t eat too many.
    • Nuts can also be eaten as a snack and sprinkled on top of other dishes such as yoghurt
  4. Fish
    • Fish can be a great source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids, which also can help reduce the risk of heart disease. Salmon can is also high in proteins, B vitamins, potassium and selenium.
    • Fish can easily be eaten at lunch or dinner as a central part of a meal or as a component of another
  5. Leafy Greens
    • Leafy greens, preferably dark ones, are a good source of vitamin A, vitamin C, calcium and fibre.
    • You can have leafy greens as sides or a salad or even in a soup
  6. Cruciferous Vegetables
    • Such as broccoli, cabbage, kale, radishes etc, are great sources of fibre, vitamins and phytochemicals.
    • You can have cruciferous vegetables as a side steamed, in a stir fry, pasta, casseroles and soups
  7. Beans
    • Beans are another great source of B vitamins, various minerals, proteins and fibre. There has also been research to show that they can reduce blood pressure and cholesterol. Additionally, they have an ability to make you feel full so you don’t end up eating as much as normal.
    • You can have beans in salads, soups, casseroles, chilli, hummus, and bean burgers
  8. Tomatoes
    • Tomatoes are rich in vitamin C and lycopene which is believed to help reduce the risk of prostate cancer
    • They can easily be put into a salad, a pasta, a soup, a stew or even just a hand full of cherry tomatoes as a snack.
  9. Olive Oil
    • Olive oil contains a lot of vitamin E, polyphenols and monosaturated fatty acids, all of which can help reduce the risk of heart disease.
    • You can use olive oil to replace butter in places such as pasta and rice dishes. You can also have it on a salad or as a dressing.
  10. Eggs
    • Eggs are full of loads of nutrients including B vitamins, choline, selenium, vitamins A and iron. They are also full of good quality proteins and antioxidants.
    • Eggs can be had in many different ways such as scrambled, poached, fried etc, but can also be had with salads.

Dinner meals 1) Broccoli & salmon bake Ingredients: 250g whole grain pasta 300g broccoli 25g butter 25g plain flour 600ml milk 100g mascarpone 8 sundried tomatoes (preserved in olive oil), drained and thickly sliced 2 tbsp small capers rinsed to remove excess salt or vinegar 8 anchovy fillets, halved (optional) 10 large fresh basil leaves, roughly torn 4 fresh skinless salmon fillets 50g mature cheddar, finely grated Method - Preheat the oven to 190C and get out an ovenproof dish. Meanwhile, put a large pan of water on to boil for the pasta. When it is boiling rapidly, tip in the pasta with a sprinkling of salt. Give it a stir, return to the boil and cook for 6 minutes. Add the broccoli, then return the water to the boil and cook for 4 minutes more, until the broccoli is on the firm side of just tender. Drain well. - While the pasta is cooking, put the butter, flour and milk in a large pan and heat, whisking or stirring continuously, until it thickens to make a smooth sauce. Remove from the heat and stir in the mascarpone, sun-dried tomatoes, capers (if using), anchovies (if using) and basil, then add the pasta and broccoli and season well. - Halve the salmon fillets widthways then place the pieces in a single layer on the base of the ovenproof dish. Spoon the broccoli mixture on top, then scatter with the grated cheddar. - Bake for 30 minutes until the mixture is just starting to bubble round the edges and the mixture is pale golden – don’t let it go too dark or the fish will overcook. 2) Chicken with Spinach & Beans Ingredients: 1 can stewed tomatoes 4 boneless skinless chicken breast halves 2 teaspoons dried rosemary, crushed 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon pepper 4 teaspoons olive oil 1 package fresh baby spinach 2 garlic cloves, minced 1 can cannellini beans, rinsed and drained Method - Drain the tomatoes, reserving juice. Chop the tomatoes. Pound chicken with a meat mallet to ¼ of an inch thickness. Rub with rosemary, salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat 2 teaspoons oil over medium heat. Add chicken; cook 5-6 minutes on each side or until no longer pink. Remove and keep warm. - In same pan, heat remaining oil over medium-high heat. Add spinach and garlic; cook and stir 2-3 minutes or until spinach is wilted. Stir in beans, tomatoes and reserved juice; heat through. Serve with chicken. 3) Spicy Beef & Pepper Stir-Fry Ingredients: 1 pound beef top sirloin steak, cut into thin strips 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger 3 garlic cloves, minced, divided 1/4 teaspoon pepper 3/4 teaspoon salt, divided 1 cup light coconut milk 2 tablespoons sugar 1 tablespoon Sriracha chili sauce 1/2 teaspoon grated lime zest 2 tablespoons lime juice 2 tablespoons canola oil, divided 1 large sweet red pepper, cut into thin strips 1/2 medium red onion, thinly sliced 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and thinly sliced 4 cups fresh baby spinach 2 green onions, thinly sliced 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro Method - In a large bowl, toss beef with ginger, 2 garlic cloves, pepper and 1/2 teaspoon salt; let stand 15 minutes. In a small bowl, whisk coconut milk, sugar, chili sauce, lime zest, lime juice and remaining salt until blended. - In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium-high heat. Add beef; stir-fry until no longer pink, 2-3 minutes. Remove from pan. - Stir-fry red pepper, red onion, jalapeno and remaining garlic in remaining oil just until vegetables are crisp-tender, 2-3 minutes. Stir in coconut milk mixture; heat through. Add spinach and beef; cook until spinach is wilted and beef is heated through, stirring occasionally. Sprinkle with green onions and cilantro.
